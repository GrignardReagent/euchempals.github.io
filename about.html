About Me

Hello! I'm Xi Yang (Ian), a passionate Computational Chemist and an aspiring expert in the integration of artificial intelligence with chemistry. I am currently a Master's student in Bioinformatics at the University of Edinburgh, and I am working towards my goal of pursuing a PhD in computational chemistry, specifically in the field of Computer-Aided Drug Design (CADD).

Education

I hold an MChem in Medicinal and Biological Chemistry from the University of Edinburgh, where I developed a strong foundation in both the theoretical and practical aspects of chemistry and drug design. My current studies in Bioinformatics are building my skills in data analysis and the application of machine learning techniques in biological data.

Professional Experience

As a Peer Support Student Coordinator (similar to a Project Coordinator) at Edinburgh's School of Chemistry, I lead a team to develop innovative peer support models that assist students in their academic and personal development. This role has honed my skills in project management, teamwork, and leadership.

Research and Projects

My research interests lie at the intersection of chemistry, biology and AI. I have worked on several projects across different fields of chemistry, including the development of 2 sustainable large-scale pharmaceutical processes that eliminates the use of hazardous reagents by a wopping annual amount of 230,000 litres; the development of 2 bioinformatics programmes using Python and Bash; and currnetly I am working on the pipeline development for a diffusion model for molecular generation under the background of antimicrobial resistance.

Future Goals

Looking forward, I aim to contribute to research in using diffusion model for molecular generation in drug discovery, with a particular focus on antimicrobial resistance. I am particularly interested in opportunities that allow me to combine my expertise in chemistry and bioinformatics to solve real-world problems.
